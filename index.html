SPACE

Space is the vast, seemingly endless area beyond Earth’s atmosphere. It starts about 100 km above us, where the air becomes too thin for planes to fly or people to breathe. Unlike Earth, space has no air, no weather, and no sound. It’s mostly empty, but it contains stars, planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and galaxies.

Choose your adventure

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and the fifth from the Sun. It is a gas giant mostly made of hydrogen and helium. Jupiter has a giant storm called the Great Red Spot, which has lasted for centuries, and it has at least 90 known moons.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is known for its pale blue-green color, caused by methane in its atmosphere. Unlike other planets, Uranus rotates on its side, making its seasons extreme. It is an ice giant with rings and at least 27 known moons.

Black hole

A black hole is a place in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. Most black holes form when massive stars collapse. They can grow larger by pulling in gas, stars, or even other black holes, and they remain one of space’s greatest mysteries.
